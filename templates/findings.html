Findings

September 2, 2013

I created this site, in part, to investigate the link between investment returns and the number of thread replies / views. I have found little predictive value of thread activity on investment performance.

Below are scatterplots of replies or views along the x axis and investment performance along the y axis. The two plots look nearly identical. (It stands to reason--a thread with the most replies is likely to have the most views as well.)

One interesting thing to note is the presence of a few significant outliers in terms of amount of thread activity. Returns for many of these outliers has been either very poor or above average, but by no means exceptional. For reference, the simple average return to date of the entire dataset is approximately 32%.

At first glance, the remaining datapoints appear to show little relationship between thread activity and returns. However, eyeballing the charts, one actually sees that, ignoring the outliers, there appears to be an inverse relationship between thread activity and the absolute value of returns. To restate more clearly, those threads with either exceptionally strong or exceptionally poor performance actually appear to have some of the least activity.
